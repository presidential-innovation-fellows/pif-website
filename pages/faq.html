Frequently Asked Questions

Q: How long is the Fellowship?

A: The Presidential Innovation Fellowship is a 12-month program, during which a Fellow will work on 2 to 3 different projects across federal agencies.

Q: Where is the Fellowship located?

A: Presidential Innovation Fellows are based in Washington, D.C. area for the duration of the Fellowship. Fellows will spend a portion of their time working at one of the federal agencies, which are typically in Washington D.C. or the surrounding areas. Additoinally, Fellows co-work and collaborate on projects in space provided by the General Services Administration.

Q: Do Fellows receive a salary?

A: All Fellows receive the same salary. The positions are qualified at the GS 15 - step 1. You can see the current salary information here.

Q: Does the program provide benefits?

A: Yes, Fellows are considered full time employees, and are eligible to receive health coverage and other benefits through the General Services Administration. You can learn more here.

Q: Is a security clearance necessary?

A: Prior to receiving a final offer from the program, candidates under consideration will be asked to complete a credit check and Moderate Background Investigation. In some cases, Fellows may have to complete higher levels of clearance in order to work at certain agencies or prior to being deployed on certain projects.

Q: Where will I be working?

A: While the Presidential Innovation Fellows program is run in partnership with the White House, the program office that administers the program is housed in the General Services Administration. All Fellows are hired by the General Services Administration (GSA) which is where the Fellowship is administratively housed. Fellows are deployed to Agencies and on projects via interagency agreements.

Q: How many Fellows are there?

A: The number of Fellows throughout the year varies depending, on the needs of our partner agencies and projects we are supporting.

Q: When can I apply?

A: We accept applications throughout the year and hire on a rolling basis depending on the needs of our agency partners.

Q: What skills does the program look for?

A: The Presidential Innovation Fellows program is a competitive process that attracts thousands of interesting and capable candidates each year. Most of our Fellowship positions require ability to work in a technical environment. Many have significant experience and track records of delivering at a very high-level, are proven leaders, and innovative thinkers. We look at domain expertise, functional expertise, and many other factors, including how the overall group will work together.

Q: Does the program accept recommendations?

A: We do not consider recommendations in the application process.

Q: What do Fellows do after they leave the program?

A: Roughly half of our Fellows have taken roles in the federal government after completing their Fellowship, and many continue to play important roles in improving the delivery of IT services within government. Alums include the U.S. Deputy Chief Technology Officer; a Chief Technology Officer at the Veterans Affairs Administration; and many that helped to establish 18F at the General Services Administration. Many others have returned to the private sector or to launch exciting companies as entrepreneurs.
